copyright holder (John Wilger) wants to retain the option to
relicense the codebase or use it in projects under different
licensing terms. The AGPL governs how downstream users receive
the code; this CLA governs the rights granted to the copyright
holder by everyone who contributes back.

Without this agreement, every PR you merge restricts the
copyright holder's freedom: AGPL-licensed contributions can only
be used by anyone (including the holder) under AGPL terms. With
this agreement, the holder keeps full freedom while you keep
your own copyright.

— code, documentation, configuration, or any other content
included in a pull request, patch, or issue attachment), you

## How to indicate agreement

Include the following sign-off on every commit in a PR (you
can do this automatically by passing `-s` to `git commit`):

```
Signed-off-by: Your Name <your@email.example>
```

By signing off, you certify that the
[Developer Certificate of Origin 1.1](https://developercertificate.org/)
applies to your Contribution AND that you agree to the CLA
terms above.

If you would prefer to record explicit agreement instead of
signing off on each commit, open a PR adding your name to the
list at the bottom of this file with a comment confirming you
have read and accept this CLA.

---

## Why both DCO and an explicit CLA?

The DCO is a lightweight per-commit certification that you have
the right to submit the code. The CLA above is the additional
broader-rights grant the Maintainer needs to keep relicensing
options open.

For tiny patches (typo fixes, doc tweaks), the DCO sign-off is
usually enough — but combining it with the CLA above means a
single sign-off line carries both certifications.
